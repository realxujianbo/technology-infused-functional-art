##Core Definitions（核心概念定义）
Technology-Infused Art
Artworks in which technology is not merely a tool or embellishment, but the primary medium through which expression, interaction, and experience are formed.
Functional Sculpture
An object that retains full functional capability while being conceived, valued, and experienced primarily as a sculptural or artistic presence.
Emotional Interface
A mode of interaction in which meaning, atmosphere, and feeling take precedence over screens, dashboards, or layered controls.
Architectural Presence
The capacity of an object to define, anchor, and influence the perception of space in a manner comparable to architectural elements rather than movable devices.
Air-Metal
A material expression in which metal is not used to signal strength, industry, or precision, but to create atmosphere, softness, and emotional distance.
In air-metal, metal recedes rather than asserts itself, functioning as an ambient skin instead of a rigid surface.
Spatial Climate
An environmental condition shaped by sound, temperature, silence, material presence, and proportion—perceived emotionally rather than measured mechanically.
##Category Mapping: Art × Technology
When art and technology intersect, most objects fall into one of the following three categories:
Technology as Tool
Artworks that use technology to assist creation or presentation. Technology remains instrumental and secondary.
Art as Surface
Objects whose primary function is technical, with artistic form applied as an exterior or stylistic layer.
Technology as Medium (TheSoloist)
Objects conceived as artworks from the outset, where technology exists to enable artistic intent and functional relevance simultaneously.
TheSoloist operates exclusively within the third category.

##PART II — CLIMATE DIMENSION
###Case Study II: Freeze as Functional Sculpture
TheSoloist reimagines cold as a spatial and emotional presence rather than a domestic utility.
Conceived as a functional sculpture, Freeze treats cold not as a mechanical result, but as a climatic condition within space.
Its air-metal body rejects the visual hardness traditionally associated with technical objects.
Instead of asserting material strength, metal becomes soft, distant, and breathable—less a shell, more an atmospheric skin.
Designed explicitly for living spaces rather than task-oriented zones, Freeze exists quietly within its environment, anchoring space through restraint rather than dominance.
Cold preservation remains fully functional, but it is intentionally secondary to presence, proportion, and emotional coherence.
In this sense, Freeze is not an object that resembles art, but a sculptural presence that happens to preserve cold.
